Green Cloud Data Center with AI Optimization - Presentation Data Pack

1) TITLE
Green Cloud Data Center with AI Optimization
AI-Driven Scheduler Comparison for Energy-Efficient Cloud Operations

2) PROBLEM STATEMENT
- Cloud data centers consume high energy and produce significant carbon emissions.
- Traditional schedulers usually optimize task completion, not sustainability.
- Heterogeneous resources (CPU, GPU, RAM, I/O) make placement optimization difficult.
- Need a scheduler that balances energy, carbon, QoS, and throughput.

3) OBJECTIVES
- Build a realistic cloud simulation platform.
- Compare 8 scheduling algorithms under identical conditions.
- Evaluate energy, carbon, SLA, latency, and utilization.
- Develop and test RLPA (Reinforcement Learning + Predictive Analytics).

4) SYSTEM OVERVIEW
- Workload arrivals generate VM requests.
- Scheduler assigns VMs to servers.
- Simulation executes placement, execution, migration, completion, and power-state events.
- Metrics module tracks energy, power, SLA, utilization, and carbon.
- Frontend provides live run visualization and algorithm comparison.

5) ALGORITHMS COMPARED
- FCFS
- RoundRobin
- BestFit
- EnergyAware
- SJF
- ACO
- GWO
- RLPA

6) EXPERIMENTAL CONFIGURATION
- Simulation time: 10,000 units
- Random seed: 42
- Workload pattern: Poisson
- Arrival rate: 5 requests per 100 time units
- Service time: mean 100, std 30
- SLA max wait threshold: 50
- VM migration: Enabled
- Server power-state control: Enabled
- Infrastructure: 5 heterogeneous servers (CPU/GPU/Balanced mix)

7) METRICS EVALUATED
- Total energy (kWh)
- Carbon footprint (kg CO2e)
- Average power (W)
- Average waiting time
- Average turnaround time
- SLA violation rate
- Average utilization
- Total migrations
- VMs completed

9) KEY FINDINGS
- RLPA has the lowest total energy among all compared schedulers.
- RLPA has the lowest carbon footprint among all compared schedulers.
- RLPA has the lowest average power draw.
- Throughput is identical for all schedulers in this setup (664 completed VMs).
- RoundRobin has the highest energy, highest carbon, and highest migration overhead.

10) PERFORMANCE DELTAS (RLPA VS OTHERS)
- Energy reduction vs RoundRobin: 22.24%
- Energy reduction vs GWO: 7.71%
- Energy reduction vs ACO: 2.76%
- Carbon reduction vs RoundRobin: 22.24%
- Carbon reduction vs GWO: 7.71%

11) RLPA METHOD SUMMARY
- Consolidation-first heuristic core.
- Predictive inputs include queue pressure and renewable availability.
- Adaptive placement strategy changes by workload pressure.
- Hybrid scoring: 95% heuristic + 5% RL correction.
- Deterministic inference mode used for reproducible comparisons.

12) INTERPRETATION
- RLPA is strongest when objective is sustainability (energy/carbon).
- BestFit yields higher utilization but slightly higher energy than RLPA.
- RoundRobin improves queue waiting but incurs major energy and migration cost.
- ACO and GWO are competitive but do not outperform RLPA on energy/carbon.

13) LIMITATION
- SLA violation is very high for all algorithms (~94-95%).
- Current SLA threshold appears stricter than job service-time distribution.
- This reduces SLA metric discrimination across algorithms in current setup.

14) CONCLUSION
- Project delivers a complete green-cloud scheduling simulation framework.
- RLPA is best performer for energy and carbon efficiency in benchmark scenario.
- Equal throughput with reduced energy indicates practical sustainability gains.
- Framework is suitable for further policy tuning and larger-scale studies.
